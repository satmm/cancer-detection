Some of the symptoms of recurrent breast cancer include:

    Local Recurrence:

  • A new lump in your breast or irregular area of firmness
  • Redness or swelling of the skin in or around the breast area
  • Nipple discharge, flattening or other changes to the nipple
  • New thickening on or near the mastectomy scar

    Regional Recurrence:

  • A lump or swelling in the lymph nodes under the arm, above the collarbone, or near the breastbone
  • Swelling in the arm on the same side where the breast cancer was first found
  • Constant pain in arm and shoulder or chest

    Distant Recurrence:

  • Symptoms can vary depending on the location, and can include bone pain, numbness or weakness anywhere in the body, constant dry cough, loss of appetite, severe headache, vision problems, constant nausea, weight loss, seizures, loss of balance.
